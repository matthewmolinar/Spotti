Why target self-checkout shoplifting?

As theft continues to occur, employees may be more likely to act on implicit bias and prejudices. As a result, conflict in-store could arise, or the police could be called. For instance, according to a Tampa Bay Times analysis, there were 16,800 police calls every year in the region from Walmarts. Many of those calls were focused on theft.
Walmart uses AI software Everseen, which relies solely on AI detection of theft. According to a group of Walmart ‘Home Office’ employees who reported to Wired, “[Everseen] misidentified innocuous behavior as theft, and often failed to stop actual instances of stealing.” During actual and mistaken instances of stealing, associates are notified, and police can be called. The consequences of this are that police are placed where they are not needed, which not only wastes taxpayer dollars, but also endangers minorities and groups subject to profiling and targeting. However, with Spotti, customers are less likely to steal. According to a study by Siebenaler, S. et al. Honesty, Social Presence and Self-Service in Retail, proximity of staff and customers may be more effective than traditional security methods such as CCTV or detections.
